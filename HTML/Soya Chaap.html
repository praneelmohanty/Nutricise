Healthy Soya Chaap Masala

A very tasty take on Chaap created using a variety of rich spices and masalas.
Recipe ⇣
  • 200g soya chaap
  • oil to fry
  • 1/2 tbsp plant-based yogurt
  • 1/8 tsp red chili powder
  • 1/8 tsp turmeric
  • 1/4 tsp chaat masala
  • salt to taste
  • 1/2 onion
  • 1/2 inch ginger
  • 1 and 1/2 garlic cloves
  • 1 tomato, processed into ~ 1/4 cup tomato puree
  • 1/2 tbsp neutral oil
  • 1/4 tbsp vegan butter, optional
  • 1/2 bay leaf
  • 1/4 cinnamon stick
  • 1/2 clove
  • 1/4 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1/4 black cardamom
  • 1/2 green cardamom
  • 1/2 green chilli
  • 1/4 tsp ground coriander
  • 1/4 tsp kashmiri chilli powder
  • 1/2 cup water, could add more
  • 1/8 tsp garam masala
  • 1/4 tsp kasoori methi (dried fenugreek leaves)
  1. Take the soya chaap off of the sticks and cut into 1-inch pieces. Heat a pot or large saucepan with about half an inch of oil. Fry the chaap until golden brown and slightly crisp on all sides.
  2. Marinate the soya chaap in yogurt, red chili powder, turmeric, chaat masala, and salt. Combine and let it sit.
  3. For the masala, finely chop 1/2 of the half onion. Process the other half with all the ginger and garlic to form a coarse paste. Process the tomatoes separately for the tomato puree.
  4. Heat oil in a pot or saucepan over medium heat. Add a little butter. When it's hot, bloom the whole spices: bay leaves, cinnamon, cloves, cumin seeds, black cardamom, and green cardamom until they begin to release their aromas.
  5. Add the chopped onions and stir until translucent and slightly golden. Add the onion-ginger-garlic paste and cook for 4-5 minutes. Add the tomato puree and a big pinch of salt. Increase heat to allow the tomato juices to mostly evaporate.
  6. Add the slit green chilis, ground coriander, and kashmiri chili powder. Combine the spices with the gravy. Then adssssd about 1/2 cup of water. Cover and simmer until it is at the texture you prefer. Add more water if necessary.
  7. Finish with garam masala, fenugreek leaves, and salt to taste. Add the marinated soya chaap and simmer for 10 minutes. Cover to maintain the texture of the gravy.
